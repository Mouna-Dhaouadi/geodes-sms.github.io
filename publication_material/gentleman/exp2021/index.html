Modelling with a projectional editor

Jetbrains MPS and Gentleman

In this experiment, you will accomplish several modelling tasks using the projectional editors Gentleman and Jetbrains MPS.

Experiment

In the following tasks, we will create, manipulate and evaluate traffic light (TL) configurations.
After the tasks, we will take a short survey to gather some additional feedback regarding your experience.

Protocol (PDF)

Task 1: Design a model

Create a TL model with the following three lights: Go (color) as GREEN (name), Hurry as YELLOW and Stop as RED.

  • Stop behaviours
    • [temporal: after 15 s] --> Go
  • Hurry behaviours
    • [temporal: after 5 s] --> Stop
  • Go behaviours
    • [temporal: after 30 s] --> Hurry

Results

Considering the mean, participants performed better using Gentleman than MPS, but with various degrees. The most important differences are found in the mechanical effort and completion time.

  • NB_Selection: 10 more with MPS than with Gentleman.
  • NB_Blank: 3 more with MPS than with Gentleman.
  • NB_CtxChange: 8 occurences with MPS and none with Gentleman.
  • T_Total: 90 more seconds to complete the task with MPS than with Gentleman.
  • NB_Block: 2 occurences with MPS and none with Gentleman.

Task 2: Evolve a model

Add the two following modes to the model created in task 1.

Mode Normal

  • Lights: same as those created in task 1
  • Initial light (start): Stop
  • Behaviours
    • [manual: On signal] --> Police

Mode Police

  • Lights: ON as YELLOW and OFF as BLACK
    • ON behaviours
      • [temporal: after 2 s] --> OFF
    • OFF behaviours
      • [temporal: after 2 s] --> OFF
  • Initial light (start): ON
  • Behaviours
    • [manual: On signal] --> Normal

Results

Except for NB_Miss, every other variable indicated a better performance with Gentleman than MPS. However, in terms of mechanical effort, the difference between the two has reduced.

  • NB_Selection: 6 more with MPS than with Gentleman.
  • NB_Blank: 3 more with MPS than with Gentleman.
  • NB_CtxChange: 10 occurences with MPS and 1 with Gentleman.
  • T_Total: 60 more seconds to complete the task with MPS than with Gentleman.
  • NB_Block: 2 occurences with MPS and none with Gentleman.

Task 3: Evaluate a model

Evaluate an existing TL model using the following requirements

  • Mode: Automatic
  • Lights: GL as GREEN, YL as YELLOW and RL as RED
  • Initial light: GL
  • Allowed behaviours:
    • GL --> YL
    • YL --> RL
    • RL --> GL
  • No manual behaviours

1. Identify the errors found in the model.
2. Apply the changes to respect the requirements.

Results

There is very little difference between MPS and Gentleman, with most variables giving a slight edge to Gentleman. During this task, a participant triggered an internal error (NB_Bug) in Gentleman and none in MPS.

Feeback survey

With the survey, we've identified the participants background and collected their subjective evaluations of Gentleman and MPS.
Participants profile

Questions – Modeling experience

Those questions were answered using a five-point Likert scale with 1 indicating strong disagreement with the statement and 5 indicating complete agreement.

  1. It is easy to understand the user interface of the editor
  2. It is easy to remember the controls and commands of the editor
  3. It is easy to know the state (values) of the model and whether it is valid
  4. It is easy to navigate through elements of a model with the keyboard
  5. It is easy to navigate through elements of a model with the mouse
  6. It is easy to reuse values across different elements of the model
  7. It is easy to know which element type (concept) is being focused on
  8. It is easy to recover from a mistake
  9. It is easy to know what actions are expected from me or available to me at any point in time
  10. It is easy to execute the necessary steps to realize an action

Results

For Gentleman, most participants strongly agreed with the statements. The average score is greater than 4.5, and the minimum is between 3 and 4. For MPS, the average score was a point lower, indicating simple agreement with the statements. The minimum for MPS mainly oscillated between 2 and 3.

  • User Interface (Q1): Gentleman: 4.73 MPS: 3.95
  • Control (Q2): Gentleman: 4.77 MPS: 4.14
  • State (Q3): Gentleman: 4.73 MPS: 4.27
  • Mouse Navigation (Q5): Gentleman: 4.86 MPS: 4.05
  • Actions (Q9): Gentleman: 4.73 MPS: 3.55
  • Executions (Q10): Gentleman: 4.77 MPS: 4.05
